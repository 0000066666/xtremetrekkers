		<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #000000;">About the place</span></h2>
<div id="stcpDiv">
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"><b>Rajmachi (Udhewadi)</b> is a small village in the rugged mountains of Sahyadri in India. The fortification at Rajmachi consists of two forts, namely Shrivardhan fort and Manaranjan Fort. Rajmachi Fort is a popular trekking destination. It is a very famous fort located near two famous hills of Maharashtra, Lonavala and Khanadala.It is about <strong>15 km treking distance from Lonavala</strong>.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">Historically, Rajmachi fort was a strategic fort to control Borghat(ghat between Khopoli and Khandala on Mumbai-Pune route) which was a historical trade route. Rajmachi peak has two fortified structures &#8211; the Shrivardhan fort and Manaranjan fort. Rajmachi can be reached by 2 ways, the tough route is from Kondivade village near Karjat and it involves climb-up of about 2000 feet. The other route is from Lonavala, which is almost a plain walk of about 15 km. In rainy season this region all the more beautiful with silvery water falls, streams and lush green forests and meadows.</span></p>
</div>
<hr />

<hr />
<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #000000;">Trekking</span></h2>
<div id="stcpDiv"><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">There are two ways to reach the Rajmachi fort. One is from Lonavla, roughly 20 km flat walk and the other is from Karjat, gradually climb of around 4 km. For beginners, it would be better to trek from Lonavla as this is a much easier path. After completing the trek they can descend to Karjat if they are from Mumbai or suburbs. For those trekkers who are from Pune, it would be better to trek back to Lonavla.</span></div>
<hr />

<h2 style="text-align: center;"><span style="color: #000000;">What to carry?</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"><strong> Important and Mandatory :</strong></span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;">1.Packed Lunch (To be carried with yourself).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 2. People using contacts &#8211; carry a bottle of lotion, an extra pair of spectacles.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 3. Emergency whistle.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 4. A water-proof jacket/sweater (if it begins to rain).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 5. Contact list of person, telephone no. and address ( in case of emergency ).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 6. Those wearing spectacles please carry an extra pair in strong spectacle box.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Mandatory :</strong></span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 1. A strong Back-Pack (preferably water proof).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 2. Water bottle (enough to carry at least 2 liters of water).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 3. An extra pair of socks, under garments, T-Shirt.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 4. A water-proof jacket/sweater (if it begins to rain).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 5. Bandanna ( keeps the sweat out of the eyes ) and/or Wide brimmed hat.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 6. Toothbrush &amp; toothpaste, towel, liquid soap.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 7. Few old news papers ( may be 4 would be fine).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 8. A torch with two new batteries and extra bulb.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 9. Some strong adhesive tape and/or A small role of strong string.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 10. A small pocket knife ( if you have a Victorinox -SWISS ARMY knife then great..).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 11. One matchbox/ lighter.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 12. Some tissue/toilet paper.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 13. Insect repellant (ex. Odomos).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 14. Basic First Aid &amp; Medicine &#8211;</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> Anti-Dysentery Pills (Eldoper)</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> Some generic pills (Crocin, Imol, etc)</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> Anti Cold preparation (COLDACT).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> Band aids.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> Gauze roll &amp; antiseptic lotion.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 15. Energy Food ( Chocolate bars, Flavored milk, Electrolyte drinks &#8211; Glucon-D, etc ).</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 16. Garbage bag to collect any litter we may create (or other people have created).</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Optional</strong><u>:<br />
</u>1. Compass &amp; Terrain Map.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 2. Camera.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 3. Extra food.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 4. Book and pen to keep trek-journal entries.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 5. A walking staff.</span><br />
<span style="color: #000000; font-size: 14pt;"> 6. Extra laces for your shoes</span></p>
